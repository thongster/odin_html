Ca Kho (Vietnamese Caramelized Fish in Clay Pot)

Home

Description

Cá Kho Tộ is a classic Southern Vietnamese dish featuring fish braised in a clay pot with a savory-sweet caramel sauce. Traditionally made with catfish, the dish develops rich flavor as the fish slowly simmers with fish sauce, sugar, garlic, and black pepper. The clay pot helps concentrate the flavors and gives the dish its signature taste, but a regular pot works too.

It’s usually served with steamed jasmine rice, fresh vegetables, and sometimes a side of soup. The tender, flavorful fish coated in glossy caramel sauce is a staple comfort food in many Vietnamese households.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb catfish steaks (or other firm fish like basa or salmon)
  • 2–3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1–2 tablespoons soy sauce (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (for caramel)
  • 1 small onion or shallot (sliced)
  • 2–3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1–2 bird’s eye chilies (optional, for spice)
  • Freshly cracked black pepper
  • 1 cup water or coconut water
  • A few green onions (cut into 2-inch pieces)

Steps

  1. Rinse fish, pat dry, and season lightly with fish sauce and pepper.
  2. Make caramel in a clay pot (or pan) by heating sugar until golden, then add a splash of water.
  3. Add garlic, shallot, and chilies, then carefully place fish pieces into the pot.
  4. Pour in fish sauce, soy sauce (if using), and coconut water.
  5. Simmer gently over low heat for 30–45 minutes, spooning sauce over fish occasionally.
  6. Add green onions near the end of cooking.
  7. Finish with a sprinkle of black pepper.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice.
