Canh Chua (Vietnamese Sweet & Sour Soup)

Home

Description

Canh Chua is a refreshing Vietnamese sweet and sour soup, often made with fish, tamarind, pineapple, and a mix of herbs and vegetables. The broth is light yet flavorful, balancing sourness from tamarind, sweetness from pineapple, and savoriness from fish sauce.

It’s a signature dish in Southern Vietnam, often enjoyed alongside caramelized dishes like Cá Kho Tộ or Thịt Kho. Canh Chua is typically served with rice and is known for being both comforting and palate-cleansing.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb catfish steaks (or other firm white fish)
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste (or tamarind concentrate)
  • 1–2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1–2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks
  • 1 medium tomato (cut into wedges)
  • 1 handful bean sprouts
  • 1–2 okra (sliced, optional)
  • 1 chili pepper (optional, for spice)
  • Fresh herbs: rice paddy herb (ngò om) or cilantro
  • Green onions (sliced)

Steps

  1. Bring water to a boil and add tamarind paste, stirring until dissolved. Strain if necessary.
  2. Add fish sauce, sugar, and taste to balance sour/sweet/savory flavors.
  3. Add pineapple, tomato, and okra, simmering until just tender.
  4. Gently add fish pieces and cook until done (about 10 minutes).
  5. Add bean sprouts and chili near the end of cooking.
  6. Sprinkle in herbs and green onions before serving.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice.
