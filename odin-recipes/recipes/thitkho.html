Thit Kho (Vietnamese Caramelized Pork)

Home

Description

Thịt Kho Tiêu is a classic Vietnamese comfort dish made with pork braised slowly in a savory-sweet caramel sauce with plenty of black pepper. Unlike the festive version with eggs, this everyday version focuses on the tender, flavorful pork. The sauce balances salty fish sauce, a touch of sweetness, and the warmth of pepper. It’s simple to prepare and pairs perfectly with steamed jasmine rice and fresh vegetables.

This dish is loved for its rich, glossy caramelized flavor and how easily it comes together with just a few pantry staples. It’s a staple in many Vietnamese households.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork belly or pork shoulder (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 2–3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1–2 tablespoons soy sauce (optional)
  • 1–2 tablespoons sugar (for caramel)
  • 1 small onion or shallot (sliced)
  • 2–3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • Freshly cracked black pepper (generous amount)
  • 1 cup water or coconut water
  • A pinch of salt (to taste)

Steps

  1. Cut pork into chunks and season lightly with fish sauce and pepper.
  2. In a pot, make caramel by heating sugar until golden, then add a little water.
  3. Add garlic, onion, and the pork pieces. Stir well to coat in the caramel.
  4. Pour in fish sauce, soy sauce (if using), and water. Stir to combine.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
  6. Let it cook on low heat for 30–45 minutes, until the pork is tender and the sauce thickens.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with more fish sauce or sugar if needed.
  8. Finish with a generous amount of freshly cracked black pepper.
  9. Serve hot with steamed jasmine rice.
